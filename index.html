UrbanSnow

Snowpack Simulation for Urban Environments

Authors: Nitin Kanchinadam, Sophie Tsai
Emails: nitin.kanchinadam@gmail.com, stsai123@umd.edu

Abstract

Snowpack in urban environments presents unique challenges and significance due to heterogeneous surface types and complex wind patterns, impacting water management, hazard prediction, and infrastructure. While existing snowpack models like SnowModel and SNOWPACK are powerful, they often struggle to capture urban-specific complexities. UrbanSnow is a snowpack simulation model designed to simulate snow accumulation and melt in urban environments. This project developed a custom, modular snowpack simulation model within the Unity game engine. e model incorporates fundamental mass balance processes, including snow accumulation based on temperature and melting simulated via both a simple Degree-Day method and a more detailed Full Energy Balance approach, accounting for varied urban surface types. While the current model has limitations, including simplified parameterizations and the omission of other snow processes, it represents a step towards building more tailored snowpack models for complex urban landscapes and highlights the insights gained from custom model development and adaptive research.
